Looking for a postdoc starting January 2026!

I am a fourth year PhD student at the University of Bern, working with Walter Senn, Mihai Petrovici and Katharina Wilmes. Before that, I studied Neural Systems and Computation (MSc) at the Institute of Neuroinformatics in Zürich. My interest is in deciphiring cortical computation, with the working hypothesis that it is based on a canonical motif repeated across cortical areas and mammalian species (Kevin Martin).

Cortical circuits are composed of different cell types fulfilling distinct computational roles, and cortical connectivity can be efficiently recapitulated with a few blueprints of connnectivity between cell types and synaptic learning rules. I believe, these are structural constraints on the form our models should take (Matthew Cook). For a few years my focus has been on normative energy-based models (Walter Senn). From this period I keep a recreational interest in the application of differential geometry to energy-based models in neuroscience. My focus is now on a more bottom-up approach, with (i) the integration of emerging experimental data on this topic and (ii) the application of automatic search in the space of cell type connectivity, looking for functional architectures.

Other interests include existentialist philosophy, in particular Camus, and cyberpunk and surreal animation cinema.
